Roberts Bank Terminal 2 Project

Potential Draft Conditions on the Fisheries Act Authorization Application for the Roberts Bank Terminal 2 (RBT2) Project as submitted by the Vancouver Fraser Port Authority (the Proponent).

Project Description

The proposed Roberts Bank Terminal 2 Project (the Project) includes construction and operation of a marine terminal, and construction of an extended tug basin, and widened causeway. The proposed Project is located adjacent to the existing Roberts Bank terminals.

The Project underwent an environmental assessment by Review Panel under the Canadian Environmental Assessment Act, 2012, with a decision made by the Governor in Council to allow the Project to proceed to permitting subject to conditions on April 20, 2024. The Project is now in the regulatory phase and DFO is conducting a review of the Application for a Species at Risk compliant Fisheries Act Authorization (FAA).

DFO is of the view that the Project as proposed is likely to result in the death of fish by means other than fishing and the harmful alteration, disruption or destruction (HADD) of fish habitat. If issued, an FAA would allow the carrying on of works, undertakings or activities that would result in the death of fish by means other than fishing and/or HADD of fish habitat, including the destruction of ciritical habitat of Southern Resident Killer Whales (SRKW).

This page provides official PDF documents of potential draft conditions on the FAA Application for stakeholders to review. Materials are unclassified and provided for reference. Please share links, or downloaded files directly with the intended recipients.
